\begin{abstract}
  Suppose that we place $n$ balls into $n$ bins by putting each ball into a bin
  chosen independently and uniformly at random. It turns out that there are
  at most $O\bigl(\log(n) / \log\bigl(\log(n)\bigr)\bigr)$ balls in each
  bin with high probability. If instead of one we choose two bins independently
  and uniformly and place each ball in the
  least loaded of the two we get at most $O\bigr(\log\bigl(\log(n)\bigr)\bigr)$
  balls in each bin with high probability. This scheme found many applications
  and we will briefly expose two of them: load balancing and PRAM simulation.
\keywords{Hashing \and Load Balancing \and PRAM Simulation \and Balls and Bins}
\end{abstract}
